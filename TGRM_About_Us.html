John W. James — Our Founder’s Story
Our Story

From Personal Heartbreak to a Global Movement

The Grief Recovery Institute was founded in the mid-1980s by John W. James after the devastating loss of his infant son. Unable to find effective help, John set out to create a structured, action-based approach to grief recovery that actually worked.

What began as one man’s search for answers has grown into the only evidence-based grief recovery program in the world, with over 3,000 Certified Grief Recovery Specialists across 6 countries.

Our Founders

John W. James

Creator of The Grief Recovery Method · Co-Author of The Grief Recovery Handbook

John W. James created The Grief Recovery Method after the death of his infant son in 1977. Unable to find effective help for his overwhelming grief, John made it his life’s mission to develop a structured, action-based program that would give grievers the tools they actually needed to recover.

His work resulted in The Grief Recovery Handbook, which has sold over one million copies worldwide and has been translated into multiple languages. John’s vision transformed grief recovery from a concept into a practical, evidence-based reality.

“We are not born with the skills to deal with loss. We must learn them.” — John W. James, Founder
RF
Co-Founder

Russell Friedman

Co-Founder, The Grief Recovery Institute · Co-Author of The Grief Recovery Handbook

Russell Friedman joined the Grief Recovery Institute in 1986 after his own experience with devastating loss following his second divorce and bankruptcy led him to discover the power of the Grief Recovery Method. What began as a personal journey of healing became a lifelong partnership with John W. James that would change the landscape of grief recovery forever.

Together, Russell and John co-authored The Grief Recovery Handbook, When Children Grieve, and Moving On — books that have helped millions of people worldwide find a path through grief. Russell became the go-to grief recovery expert for CNN, appearing on the network multiple times, most notably in the aftermath of 9/11. He also appeared on The Today Show and consulted with major companies on the impact of unresolved loss in the workplace.

Through Russell’s tireless work alongside John, the Grief Recovery Method Support Group grew to help more than 500,000 people across the country and internationally.

Russell dedicated his life to ensuring no one had to grieve alone. His warmth, wisdom, and unwavering belief in the power of human connection touched every life he encountered.
🕒 1943 – 2016 · Forever in our hearts

What Guides Us

Our Core Values

Every decision we make is guided by these principles.

💜

Action Over Time

We believe grief recovery requires specific actions, not just the passage of time. Our evidence-based program gives people the concrete tools they need to move forward.

🤝

A Heart with Ears

Our founder’s guiding philosophy: being truly present, listening without judgment, and creating safe spaces where people feel heard and understood.

🌎

Global Accessibility

Grief is universal. We’re committed to making evidence-based grief recovery accessible to everyone, everywhere — across 6 countries and growing.
