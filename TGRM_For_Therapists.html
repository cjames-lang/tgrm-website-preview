The Gap in Your Training

You were trained to treat anxiety, depression, and trauma. But what about grief?

“Even professionals have admitted that their training fell short when it came to grief.”

Most graduate programs dedicate a few hours — at most — to grief and loss. You learn the Kübler-Ross stages. You learn to “hold space.” But when a client sits across from you, stuck in grief for months or years, and asks “What do I actually do with this pain?” — the answer often isn’t there.

That gap isn’t your fault. It’s a gap in the training. And it affects every clinician who works with human beings — because grief touches every life.

Theory Without Action

You know the stages and models. But you don’t have a step-by-step method to guide a client from pain to completion.

Death-Focused Only

Most grief training covers bereavement. But clients grieve divorce, job loss, health changes, estrangement — over 40 types of loss.

Support ≠ Solution

Holding space is essential. But it’s not enough. Clients need structured action steps that lead to resolution — not just coping.

Common Questions from Clinicians

We know you’ll have questions. Here are the ones we hear most.

How does GRM integrate with my existing clinical practice?
Many therapists use GRM as a structured grief intervention alongside their primary modality — whether that’s CBT, EMDR, psychodynamic, or another approach. The method gives you a clear, action-based protocol for grief-specific work that complements what you already do. Clients often make faster progress in their broader therapy after completing the GRM program.
Is this approved for CE credits?
The certification is approved for Veteran’s Education Benefits and may be used for continuing education credit with the Association of Professional Chaplains. For other license types, we recommend checking with your state board, as CE acceptance varies. Many licensed professionals attend because the practical tools fill a gap that standard CE courses don’t address.
Can I really be effective after just 4 days?
We won’t call you an expert in 4 days. But you’ll leave with a solid foundation, every program you need to start, and the personal experience of having gone through the method yourself. Combined with your existing clinical skills, most therapists tell us they feel ready to offer their first program within weeks. And you’ll have a year of unlimited support from our team as you build confidence.
How is this different from a PESI or Evergreen grief course?
Those are excellent CE programs — and many of our specialists hold them too. The difference is output: CE courses teach you about grief, then you build your own approach. GRM certifies you to deliver a complete, structured program with ready-made lesson plans, homework assignments, and a step-by-step process. Plus we’re the only program with evidence-based validation from Kent State University.
Will my clients take this seriously if it’s not psychotherapy?
Yes. The GRM is positioned as an educational program, which actually lowers the barrier for many clients who are resistant to “therapy.” Therapists across the country report that clients who complete the GRM program make remarkable progress in their broader therapeutic work afterward. It’s a complement to your practice, not a replacement.
