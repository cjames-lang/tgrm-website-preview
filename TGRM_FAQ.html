Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about becoming a Certified Grief Recovery Specialist® — from people who asked the same questions before they enrolled.

Getting Started Cost & Payment The Training After Certification For Specific Audiences
🎯 Getting Started
Do I need a degree, license, or any credentials to get certified?
No. Our certification is open to anyone with a genuine desire to help others. Many of our most effective specialists started as grievers themselves — people who went through their own recovery and felt called to help others do the same. Therapists, coaches, nurses, chaplains, and people with no formal credentials all train side by side. Your willingness to learn is the only prerequisite.
I'm not a professional — I'm grieving. Is this training for me?
Many people come to us looking for personal grief help and discover that the certification training is exactly what they need. You'll do your own grief recovery work during the 4 days, so it's both deeply personal healing AND professional training. Some of our best specialists are people who found the method as grievers and decided to help others. If you're looking for personal help only, we also offer a Free online course, the Grief Recovery Handbook, and a directory of 3,000+ certified specialists.
I'm interested but I'm not sure I'm ready. How do I know?
If you're asking the question, you're probably closer to ready than you think. Many specialists say they wished they hadn't waited. That said, there's no pressure — our training dates run throughout the year. If you'd like to talk it through, you can book a free call with someone on our team who can help you figure out if the timing is right.
What's the difference between the Foundational, Advanced, and Premium tracks?
Foundational ($2,495) is the core 4-day, 32-hour live certification — you get 5 program lesson plans, all 5 books, your credential, directory listing, and 1 year of support. Advanced ($3,995) adds 18 hours of self-paced video training with additional lesson plans for one-on-one programs, children's grief, and pet loss, plus 150 marketing videos. Premium In-Person ($4,995) is the Advanced content delivered in person at select locations, taught by Cole James and Sandi Derby, with small-group coaching and roleplay.
💰 Cost & Payment
How much does certification cost?
Training ranges from $2,495 to $4,995 depending on the track. Foundational is $2,495, Advanced is $3,995, and Premium In-Person is $4,995. All tracks include payment plans — 3 monthly installments with 0% interest.
Do you offer payment plans?
Yes. Every track offers a 3-payment plan: Foundational at $865/month, Advanced at $1,365/month, and Premium at $1,698/month. No credit check, no interest, processed through Stripe.
Can my employer pay for this? How do I get organizational approval?
Many specialists have their training funded by employers — hospitals, hospice organizations, VA facilities, churches, schools, and counseling practices. We can provide documentation of training hours, program outcomes, and the Kent State University research validation to support your request. If you need help making the case to your organization, book a call with our team and we'll walk you through it. We also offer private group training for organizations that want to certify multiple staff members at once.
Are there military, veteran, or VA benefits available?
The certification training is approved for Veteran's Education Benefits. If you're a veteran or active-duty service member, contact us to discuss how to apply your benefits. We also have a dedicated military & veterans track with specialists who understand service-connected grief.
Is the investment worth it?
We hear this question a lot, and the honest answer is: it depends on what you do with it. Many specialists run grief recovery groups through their church, practice, or community and find the training pays for itself quickly. Others integrate it into existing counseling, coaching, or healthcare work. But beyond the financial return — specialists consistently tell us this training changed their lives personally, not just professionally. You do your own grief recovery work during the 4 days, and that alone is worth the investment for many people.
📚 The Training
What happens during the 4 days of training?
Each day runs from 9am to 5pm with breaks. You'll learn the Grief Recovery Method by experiencing it yourself — you go through your own grief recovery process while simultaneously learning how to guide others through it. It's a mix of instruction, demonstration, guided practice, and Q&A. Cohorts are capped at 20 people for a small-group, intimate experience. Online sessions use Zoom with cameras on.
Is the online training as effective as in-person?
Yes. The online format is the same 4-day, 32-hour program with the same content, same caliber of trainer, and the same outcomes. Many specialists actually prefer the online format for the convenience and comfort of being in their own space. The only format exclusive to in-person is the Premium track ($4,995), which adds small-group coaching and roleplay with Cole James and Sandi Derby.
I'm worried the training will be too emotionally intense. What should I expect?
It IS emotional — because you're doing your own real grief recovery work. But it's also structured, safe, and guided by experienced trainers. You won't be asked to share anything you're not comfortable with. Many people describe the experience as "the best thing they've ever done" — both personally and professionally. The emotional work is what makes you an effective specialist.
What if the available dates don't work for me?
We run training sessions throughout the year across multiple time zones. If the current dates don't work, you can book a call with our team to discuss upcoming sessions. We typically announce new dates quarterly. You can also request to be notified when new dates are added.
What if I need to reschedule?
If you reschedule more than 14 days before your training date, the transfer is free. Within 14 days, there's a $200 rescheduling fee. We understand life happens and work with you to find a new date that fits.
🏆 After Certification
What can I actually do after I'm certified?
You'll receive ready-to-use lesson plans for 5 programs: an 8-session group program, a 7-session one-on-one program, a pet loss group, a helping children with loss program, and an alumni program. You can run these through your private practice, church, community center, hospice, school, VA facility, or any setting. Many specialists offer both in-person and online programs.
Will I get clients? How do I find people to help?
Your certification includes a listing in our searchable online directory — where grievers actively look for help — plus a personal microsite with your bio, specialties, and contact information. Advanced and Premium tracks also include 150 marketing videos and practice-building resources. Beyond that, you'll join a community of 3,000+ specialists who share referrals, resources, and support.
What support do I get after training?
One full year of unlimited support — email, phone, and Zoom with experienced trainers. You also get access to the specialist resource community for peer connection, ongoing training, and collaboration with 3,000+ specialists worldwide. You're never on your own.
Can I upgrade from Foundational to Advanced later?
Yes. The Advanced upgrade includes 18 additional hours of self-paced video training covering one-on-one programs, children's grief, pet loss, and marketing tools. You can upgrade at any time after completing Foundational.
👥 For Specific Audiences
I'm a therapist. How does this complement my clinical training?
Most clinical programs teach grief theory — stages, models, assessments — but don't provide a structured, action-based protocol for actually helping clients move through grief. This certification fills that gap. You'll have a concrete 7-8 session program that gives your clients specific steps to take, not just insights to discuss. It works for 40+ types of loss, not just death. And you experience it yourself during training, which gives you a depth of understanding no textbook can provide.
I'm a life coach. Can I do this work within my scope?
Absolutely. The Grief Recovery Method is action-based, not therapeutic. It gives you a clear framework that helps your coaching clients break through the grief-related blocks that are keeping them stuck — divorce, career loss, health crises, estrangement, and more. Many coaches say this is the missing piece that transformed their practice.
I'm a pastor or faith leader. Does this fit with my ministry?
Yes. The method works alongside any faith tradition — it doesn't replace your theology, it gives you practical tools to help your congregation process grief in a structured way. As one Bible teacher put it: "This isn't a new theology. It's a simple, structured way to help people process pain so they can hear God's truth more clearly." Many churches now run grief recovery groups as a core ministry.
I want to bring this to my entire organization. Is that possible?
Yes. We offer private group training for organizations — online via Zoom or in-person at your facility. Teams of 12-20 can be trained together on dates that work for your organization. This is popular with hospitals, hospice programs, VA facilities, military units, churches, and counseling practices. Visit our Private Training page or call us to discuss.
