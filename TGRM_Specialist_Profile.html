📜 About Sarah

I became a Certified Grief Recovery Specialist because I saw a gap in my clinical work that traditional therapy alone wasn't filling. After 15 years as a licensed clinical social worker, I kept encountering clients who had done the cognitive work, understood their patterns intellectually, but still carried an emotional heaviness that wouldn't lift. That heaviness was unresolved grief.

The Grief Recovery Method gave me — and now gives my clients — a structured, evidence-based path to complete the unfinished emotional business that loss leaves behind. Whether it's the death of a loved one, a divorce, a career change, the loss of a pet, or the end of a friendship, grief touches every part of life.

I specialize in working with adults and teens navigating all types of loss. I've facilitated over 200 Grief Recovery programs, both one-on-one and in group settings, and I also maintain a private therapy practice for those who benefit from ongoing clinical support alongside the GRM process.

My approach is warm, direct, and judgment-free. I believe everyone deserves a space to be heard — truly heard — without being analyzed, fixed, or told how they should feel. The GRM process provides exactly that: an action-based program that helps you move from where you are to where you want to be.

When I'm not working, you'll find me hiking the trails around the Columbia River Gorge with my two dogs, volunteering at our local food bank, or curled up with a good book and far too much coffee.
