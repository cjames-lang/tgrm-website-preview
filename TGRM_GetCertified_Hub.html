Common Questions
Frequently Asked Questions
Do I need a degree or license to get certified?
No. Our certification is open to anyone with a genuine desire to help others. Many of our most effective specialists are people who went through their own grief recovery and felt called to help others do the same. Therapists, coaches, chaplains, nurses, and people with no formal credentials all train side by side.
What happens during the 4 days of training?
You learn the method by experiencing it yourself. You'll go through your own grief recovery process while simultaneously learning how to guide others through it. It's both deeply personal and professionally transformative. Training is led by experienced senior trainers in small groups of 20 or fewer.
What can I do after certification?
You'll receive 5 complete program lesson plans: an 8-session group program, a 7-session one-on-one program, a pet loss group, a helping children with loss program, and an alumni program. You can run these through your practice, church, community center, or any setting.
Is the training available online?
Yes. We offer both live online (Zoom) and in-person training. The online format is the same 4-day, 32-hour program with the same content, same trainer, and same outcomes. Many specialists prefer the online option for flexibility.
How much does it cost?
Training ranges from $2,495 to $4,995 depending on the tier you choose. Payment plans are available (3 monthly installments). Many specialists say the investment pays for itself quickly through the work they're able to do.
What support do I get after certification?
One full year of unlimited support — email, phone, and Zoom with experienced trainers. You also get access to the specialist resource community, ongoing training opportunities, and peer connection with 3,000+ specialists worldwide.
